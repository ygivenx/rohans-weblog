I explore two profound ideas encountered over a weekend.

## What Do You Want in Life?

Drawing from Morgan Housel's *Psychology of Money*, I examine a 1968 Sunday Times competition offering £5,000 to anyone sailing solo around the world. Two competitors—Donald Crowhurst and Bernard Moitessier—exemplified how "the quality of your life is shaped by whom you want to impress."

I reflect on finding balance between competing values, recognizing that life direction depends on personal priorities chosen today.

## Embracing New Paradigms

Jeff Bezos's insight forms the core of the second reflection: "When there is a new paradigm, like the internet, you want to find things you just can't do in the old paradigm." This concept emerged from the *Acquired* podcast's extensive Amazon.com episode (3+ hours).

I consider how companies like Barnes & Noble could have thrived by fully committing to internet-based book sales, rather than clinging to traditional retail. The lesson: succeeding with new paradigms requires willingness to sacrifice short-term profitability for transformative long-term positioning.
